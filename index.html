Why it works

The Science of Hooks

01

The 3-Second Rule

Viewers decide within the first 3 seconds whether to keep watching. A hook that fails this window loses the sale — no matter how good the rest of the ad is.

02

Pattern Interrupts

The brain filters out repetitive content. Unexpected visuals, sounds or statements trigger the orienting reflex — forcing the viewer to pay attention.

03

Open Loops

The Zeigarnik effect proves people remember unfinished things. A hook that opens a question the brain can't close keeps viewers watching until the answer arrives.

04

Emotional Priming

Emotion drives decision-making before logic does. The right hook triggers curiosity, fear of missing out or desire — putting the viewer in a buying state of mind.
